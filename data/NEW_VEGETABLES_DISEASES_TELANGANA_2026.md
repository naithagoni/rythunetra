## 🌿 1. IVY GOURD (దొండ కాయ - Donda Kaya)

**Scientific Name:** _Coccinia grandis_
**Crop Type:** Perennial climbing vegetable
**Suitable Land Types:** Black soils, Red sandy loams, Alluvial soils (all well-drained soils)
**Season:** Year-round (perennial), peak harvest Kharif & Rabi
**Major Telangana Districts:** Warangal, Karimnagar, Nizamabad, Medak, Khammam, Nalgonda
**Importance/Uses:** Popular backyard vegetable, high demand in local markets, medicinal properties (anti-diabetic), tender shoots and fruits consumed

---

### FUNGAL DISEASES

#### 1.1. **Powdery Mildew** (తెల్ల కందుపూత)

- **Pathogen:** _Erysiphe cichoracearum_, _Podosphaera xanthii_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. White powdery coating on both leaf surfaces (upper surface mainly)
    2. Yellowing and curling of affected leaves
    3. Premature defoliation reducing photosynthesis
    4. Reduced vine vigor and fruit quality/yield
- **Favorable Conditions:** Dry weather with moderate temperatures (20-28°C), low to moderate humidity, poor air circulation
- **Severity:** High - Can cause 30-50% yield loss
- **Land Types Affected:** All (especially during October-February dry period)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Pseudomonas fluorescens spray** (5-8g/L water every 10 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Buttermilk + Turmeric spray** (1L buttermilk + 20g turmeric powder in 10L water) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem oil 0.3%** (3ml/L + 1ml liquid soap as emulsifier) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 1.2. **Downy Mildew** (క్రిందికందుపూత)

- **Pathogen:** _Pseudoperonospora cubensis_
- **Type:** Oomycete (fungal-like)
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellow angular spots on upper leaf surface confined by veins
    2. Gray-purple downy fungal growth on lower leaf surface
    3. Spots turn brown and necrotic, leaves become brittle
    4. Severe defoliation in 7-10 days if untreated
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (>85%), cool nights with heavy dew, rain splash, temperature 15-22°C
- **Severity:** Very High - Can destroy entire crop within 2 weeks
- **Land Types Affected:** All (severe in Rabi/winter season)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 1%** (preventive spray every 7-10 days) - **Effectiveness: Very High**
    2. **Trichoderma viride foliar spray** (5g/L water + sticker) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Panchagavya 3%** (300ml in 10L water with copper sulfate 2g) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 1.3. **Anthracnose / Fruit Rot** (యాంత్రాక్నోజ్ / పండు కుళ్ళు)

- **Pathogen:** _Colletotrichum lagenarium_, _C. gloeosporioides_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Circular water-soaked lesions on fruits with sunken centers
    2. Pink to orange spore masses in concentric rings during moist conditions
    3. Leaf spots with brown margins and gray centers
    4. Fruit drop and post-harvest decay
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, warm weather (25-30°C), prolonged leaf wetness, fruit injuries
- **Severity:** High - 20-40% fruit loss, poor marketability
- **Land Types Affected:** All (severe during monsoon)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Trichoderma harzianum spray** (5g/L every 10-12 days on fruits & foliage) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Bordeaux mixture 0.5%** (gentle spray on fruits) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Garlic-Ginger extract** (100g each crushed, boiled in 1L water, diluted 1:10) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 1.4. **Root Rot / Wilt Complex** (వేరు కుళ్ళు / వాడిపోవుట)

- **Pathogen:** _Fusarium solani_, _Rhizoctonia solani_, _Pythium_ spp.
- **Type:** Fungal (soil-borne)
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellowing of vines starting from lower leaves
    2. Wilting during hot hours, initially recovering at night, then permanent
    3. Dark brown discoloration of roots and collar region
    4. Plant death within 10-15 days, entire vine collapses
- **Favorable Conditions:** Waterlogged soils, poor drainage, continuous cultivation in same location, high soil temperature
- **Severity:** High - Individual vine loss but can spread through field
- **Land Types Affected:** Black Cotton Soils (waterlogging), poorly drained areas
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Trichoderma viride soil drench** (10g/L around root zone, repeat after 15 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Pseudomonas fluorescens** (root zone application 10g/plant mixed with FYM) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Improve drainage + Neem cake application** (200kg/acre as soil amendment) - **Effectiveness: Medium (preventive)**

## 🫘 2. BROAD BEANS (చిక్కుడు కాయ - Chikkudu Kaya / Avare Kayi)

**Scientific Name:** _Vicia faba_
**Crop Type:** Annual legume vegetable
**Suitable Land Types:** Black soils (best), Alluvial soils, Red sandy loams (with irrigation)
**Season:** Rabi (October-February) - cool season crop
**Major Telangana Districts:** Rangareddy, Medak, Nizamabad, Sangareddy, Vikarabad
**Importance/Uses:** Protein-rich vegetable, green manure crop, nitrogen fixation (enriches soil), popular in urban markets

---

### FUNGAL DISEASES

#### 2.1. **Rust** (తుప్పు వ్యాధి)

- **Pathogen:** _Uromyces viciae-fabae_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Small reddish-brown pustules (uredinia) on leaves, stems, and pods
    2. Pustules rupture releasing rust-colored spore powder
    3. Yellowing of surrounding tissue, premature leaf fall
    4. Severe infection causes complete defoliation and pod shriveling
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (>80%), moderate temperatures (15-25°C), dense planting, prolonged dew
- **Severity:** Very High - Can cause 30-70% yield loss
- **Land Types Affected:** All (especially in Rabi with high dew)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 1%** (spray at first sign, repeat every 10 days) - **Effectiveness: Very High**
    2. **Trichoderma harzianum + Pseudomonas mix** (5g each/L water) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Garlic-Chilli extract spray** (100g garlic + 50g chilli, boiled, diluted 1:8) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 2.2. **Chocolate Spot / Leaf & Pod Spot** (చాక్లెట్ మచ్చ)

- **Pathogen:** _Botrytis fabae_, _Ascochyta fabae_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Small chocolate-brown spots on leaves, enlarging to cause blighting
    2. Similar lesions on stems, flowers, and pods
    3. Infected pods develop sunken black lesions, seeds get discolored
    4. In severe cases, entire plant turns black and dies (aggressive phase)
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool moist weather (10-20°C), high humidity, nitrogen deficiency, dense canopy
- **Severity:** Very High - Most destructive disease of broad beans
- **Land Types Affected:** All (severe in high rainfall/irrigation areas)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 1%** (preventive application every 7-10 days) - **Effectiveness: Very High**
    2. **Trichoderma viride spray** (5g/L + potassium silicate 2g/L for resistance) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Panchagavya 3% + Pseudomonas** (300ml Panchagavya + 5g Pseudomonas/10L) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 2.3. **Powdery Mildew** (తెల్ల కందుపూత)

- **Pathogen:** _Erysiphe pisi_, _Leveillula taurica_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. White powdery patches on upper and lower leaf surfaces
    2. Affected leaves curl, turn yellow, and dry up
    3. Reduced pod setting and pod filling
    4. Stunted plant growth
- **Favorable Conditions:** Dry weather with moderate temperature (20-25°C), low humidity, poor air circulation
- **Severity:** Medium-High - 15-30% yield reduction
- **Land Types Affected:** Black Cotton Soils, Red Sandy Loams (dry periods)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Pseudomonas fluorescens spray** (8g/L every 10 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Buttermilk spray** (1L buttermilk in 10L water, spray weekly) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem cake extract** (1kg neem cake soaked in 10L water overnight, filtered, spray) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

### VIRAL DISEASES

#### 2.4. **Bean Leaf Roll Virus / Yellow Mosaic** (ఆకు చుట్టుట వైరస్)

- **Pathogen:** Bean leaf roll virus (BLRV), Bean yellow mosaic virus (BYMV)
- **Vector:** Aphids (_Aphis fabae_, _Acyrthosiphon pisum_)
- **Type:** Viral
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellowing and mosaic patterns on young leaves
    2. Downward rolling and thickening of leaves
    3. Severe stunting of plants
    4. Reduced flowering and pod formation, small pods
- **Favorable Conditions:** High aphid population, warm dry weather (aphid multiplication), proximity to alternate hosts
- **Severity:** High - No cure, prevention is key
- **Land Types Affected:** All
- **Organic Remedies (Vector Control):**
    1. **Neem oil 0.5%** (5ml/L spray to control aphids every 5-7 days) - **Effectiveness: High for vector control**
    2. **NSKE 5%** (50g neem seed powder/L, overnight soak, spray) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Remove infected plants immediately + yellow sticky traps + reflective mulch** - **Effectiveness: Medium (preventive)**

## 🫛 3. CLUSTER BEANS (గోరుచిక్కుడు - Goru Chikkudu)

**Scientific Name:** _Cyamopsis tetragonoloba_
**Crop Type:** Annual legume vegetable (also grown for gum)
**Suitable Land Types:** Red sandy loams (best), Black soils, Light-textured well-drained soils
**Season:** Kharif (June-October) - warm season crop
**Major Telangana Districts:** Mahabubnagar, Nagarkurnool, Rangareddy, Nalgonda, Medak
**Importance/Uses:** Vegetable (tender pods), guar gum (industrial use), green manure, drought-tolerant, improves soil health

---

### FUNGAL DISEASES

#### 3.1. **Bacterial Leaf Spot / Blight** (బాక్టీరియల్ ఆకు మచ్చ)

- **Pathogen:** _Xanthomonas cyamopsidis_
- **Type:** Bacterial
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Small water-soaked angular spots on leaves turning brown with yellow halo
    2. Spots coalesce causing large blighted areas
    3. Pod lesions as dark sunken spots reducing marketability
    4. Defoliation in severe cases
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, warm temperature (25-30°C), rain splash, overhead irrigation
- **Severity:** High - 20-40% yield loss
- **Land Types Affected:** All (severe during monsoon with heavy rains)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Pseudomonas fluorescens spray** (8-10g/L every 7-10 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Copper oxychloride 0.3% + Bordeaux mixture alternately** - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Garlic bulb extract** (100g crushed garlic boiled, diluted 1:10, spray) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 3.2. **Alternaria Leaf Spot** (ఆల్టర్నేరియా ఆకు మచ్చ)

- **Pathogen:** _Alternaria cyamopsidis_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Circular to irregular brown spots with concentric rings (target-board pattern)
    2. Spots enlarge and coalesce, causing blighting of leaves
    3. Premature defoliation starting from lower leaves
    4. Reduced photosynthesis and pod quality
- **Favorable Conditions:** Warm humid weather (24-28°C), moderate rainfall, nutrient deficiency
- **Severity:** Medium-High - 15-30% yield reduction
- **Land Types Affected:** All
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 1%** (spray at 15-day intervals) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Trichoderma viride + Pseudomonas** (5g each/L water) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem oil 0.3%** (3ml/L with sticker) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 3.3. **Powdery Mildew** (తెల్ల కందుపూత)

- **Pathogen:** _Leveillula taurica_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. White powdery growth on lower leaf surface initially, then upper surface
    2. Yellowing and drying of affected leaves
    3. Reduced pod size and quality
    4. Premature plant senescence
- **Favorable Conditions:** Dry weather with cool nights, low humidity, temperature 20-28°C
- **Severity:** Medium - 10-25% yield loss
- **Land Types Affected:** Red Sandy Loams, Black soils (during dry spells)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Pseudomonas spray** (5g/L every 10 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Buttermilk + Cow dung slurry** (1L buttermilk + 500g cow dung in 10L water) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem cake extract spray** (1kg/10L water, soaked overnight) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 3.4. **Root Rot / Wilt** (వేరు కుళ్ళు / వాడిపోవుట)

- **Pathogen:** _Rhizoctonia bataticola_ (Macrophomina), _Fusarium_ spp.
- **Type:** Fungal (soil-borne)
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellowing and wilting of leaves during hot afternoon hours
    2. Browning and rotting of tap root and lateral roots
    3. Vascular browning visible when stem is split
    4. Complete plant death within 7-14 days
- **Favorable Conditions:** High soil temperature (>30°C), moisture stress followed by irrigation, poor drainage
- **Severity:** High - Can cause patchy to widespread crop loss
- **Land Types Affected:** Red Sandy Loams (moisture stress), poorly drained Black soils
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Trichoderma viride soil application** (2.5kg/acre mixed with FYM before sowing + seedling dip) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Pseudomonas fluorescens** (seed treatment 10g/kg + soil drench 10g/L at 30 DAS) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem cake 200kg/acre** (soil incorporation + maintain optimum moisture) - **Effectiveness: Medium (preventive)**

## 🌿 4. MINT (పుదీనా - Pudina)

**Scientific Name:** _Mentha spicata_ (Spearmint), _Mentha piperita_ (Peppermint)
**Crop Type:** Perennial aromatic herb
**Suitable Land Types:** Alluvial soils (best), Red sandy loams with high organic matter, Black soils (with good drainage)
**Season:** Year-round (perennial), peak growth during Kharif & post-monsoon
**Major Telangana Districts:** Warangal, Khammam, Karimnagar, Medak, Rangareddy (kitchen gardens & commercial)
**Importance/Uses:** Culinary herb (chutneys, beverages), essential oil extraction, medicinal (digestive, respiratory), high economic value

---

### FUNGAL DISEASES

#### 4.1. **Mint Rust** (పుదీనా తుప్పు)

- **Pathogen:** _Puccinia menthae_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Small yellow spots on upper leaf surface developing into orange-brown pustules below
    2. Pustules rupture releasing orange rust-colored spores
    3. Severe infection causes complete defoliation
    4. Reduced oil content and quality (off-flavor)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (>80%), moderate temperature (18-24°C), dense planting, poor air circulation
- **Severity:** Very High - Most serious disease of mint, can cause total crop loss
- **Land Types Affected:** All (especially in humid locations)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 1%** (spray every 10-12 days as preventive) - **Effectiveness: Very High**
    2. **Trichoderma harzianum spray** (5g/L + neem oil 0.2%) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Garlic extract + Pseudomonas** (garlic extract 1:10 + 5g Pseudomonas/L) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 4.2. **Verticillium Wilt** (వర్టిసిలియమ్ వాడిపోవుట)

- **Pathogen:** _Verticillium dahliae_
- **Type:** Fungal (soil-borne, vascular)
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellowing of lower leaves progressing upward
    2. One-sided wilting of plant initially
    3. Vascular browning visible in stem cross-section
    4. Stunting and death of entire plant
- **Favorable Conditions:** Cool to moderate soil temperature (20-25°C), continuous mint cultivation, infected planting material
- **Severity:** High - Can persist in soil for years
- **Land Types Affected:** All (especially in continuous mint cultivation areas)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Trichoderma viride** (soil application 2.5kg/acre + runner/stolon treatment before planting) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Pseudomonas fluorescens** (soil drench 10g/L at 15-day intervals) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Solarization + crop rotation** (rotate with non-host crops for 2 years) - **Effectiveness: High (preventive)**

#### 4.3. **Anthracnose / Leaf Blight** (యాంత్రాక్నోజ్ / ఆకు కాలుడు)

- **Pathogen:** _Colletotrichum_ spp.
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Small brown to black angular spots on leaves
    2. Spots enlarge with purple-brown margins
    3. Leaf distortion and curling
    4. Severe cases cause stem dieback
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, warm temperature (25-30°C), overhead irrigation, wounds
- **Severity:** Medium-High - 20-35% foliage loss
- **Land Types Affected:** All
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Bordeaux mixture 0.5%** (gentle spray every 12-15 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Trichoderma viride foliar spray** (5g/L water) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Panchagavya 3%** (300ml/10L water spray) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

#### 4.4. **Powdery Mildew** (తెల్ల కందుపూత)

- **Pathogen:** _Erysiphe biocellata_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Symptoms:**
    1. White powdery coating on leaves and tender stems
    2. Leaf curling and distortion
    3. Reduced plant vigor and aromatic oil quality
    4. Yellowing and premature senescence
- **Favorable Conditions:** Moderate temperature (20-28°C), low to moderate humidity, shaded conditions
- **Severity:** Medium - 15-25% yield and quality reduction
- **Land Types Affected:** All (especially in winter/post-monsoon)
- **Organic Remedies:**
    1. **Pseudomonas fluorescens spray** (8g/L every 10 days) - **Effectiveness: High**
    2. **Buttermilk spray** (1L buttermilk + 10g turmeric in 10L water, weekly) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Neem oil 0.3%** (3ml/L + liquid soap emulsifier) - **Effectiveness: Medium**

## 🍃 5. RED SORREL / GONGURA (గోంగూర - Gongura)

**Scientific Name:** _Hibiscus sabdariffa_ var. sabdariffa
**Crop Type:** Annual leafy vegetable & condiment
**Suitable Land Types:** Red sandy loams (best), Black soils, Alluvial soils - all well-drained soils
**Season:** Kharif (June-October) - warm season crop
**Major Telangana Districts:** All districts - Most popular in Khammam, Warangal, Karimnagar, Nalgonda, Mahabubnagar

    4. Easy uprooting of affected plants

- **Land Types Affected:** Black Cotton Soils (waterlogging), heavy clay soils, low-lying areas

### VIRAL DISEASES

#### 5.5. **Yellow Vein Mosaic Virus** (పసుపు నాడి మొజాయిక్)

- **Pathogen:** Hibiscus yellow vein mosaic virus (HYVMV) - Begomovirus
- **Vector:** Whitefly (_Bemisia tabaci_)
- **Type:** Viral
- **Symptoms:**
    1. Yellowing of veins creating a network pattern on leaves
    2. Leaf puckering and distortion
    3. Stunted plant growth
    4. Reduced leaf yield and poor quality (tough texture)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High whitefly population, hot dry weather (whitefly multiplication), continuous cropping
- **Severity:** High - No cure available, prevention is critical
- **Land Types Affected:** All
- **Organic Remedies (Vector Control & Prevention):**
    1. **Neem oil 0.5%** (5ml/L spray every 5-7 days for whitefly control) - **Effectiveness: High for vector**
    2. **NSKE 5% + yellow sticky traps** (50g neem powder/L + traps @ 8-10/acre) - **Effectiveness: Medium-High**
    3. **Early removal of infected plants + reflective mulch + border crops (maize/sorghum)** - **Effectiveness: Medium (preventive)**

## GENERAL ORGANIC MANAGEMENT PRACTICES FOR ALL 5 VEGETABLES

### Cultural Practices (Preventive)

1. **Crop Rotation:** Rotate with non-related crops (cereals, millets) for 2-3 seasons
2. **Field Sanitation:** Remove and destroy infected plant parts, avoid crop residue
3. **Proper Spacing:** Ensure adequate plant spacing for air circulation
4. **Drainage:** Maintain good field drainage, avoid waterlogging
5. **Resistant Varieties:** Select locally adapted varieties with disease tolerance
6. **Certified Seeds/Planting Material:** Use disease-free seeds/runners/stolons
7. **Irrigation Management:** Avoid overhead irrigation, prefer drip irrigation
8. **Balanced Nutrition:** Apply organic manures (FYM, vermicompost), avoid excess nitrogen

### Soil Health Management

1. **Trichoderma application:** 2.5 kg/acre mixed with 100kg FYM before sowing
2. **Neem cake:** 200 kg/acre as soil amendment (nematode control + fertilizer)
3. **Pseudomonas:** 2.5 kg/acre mixed with FYM
4. **Green manuring:** Grow and incorporate dhaincha/sunhemp before vegetable cultivation
5. **Vermicompost:** 2-3 tonnes/acre for soil health

### Spray Schedule (Preventive, for Kharif Season)

- **15 DAS:** Trichoderma foliar spray (5g/L)
- **25 DAS:** Pseudomonas spray (8g/L)
- **35 DAS:** Bordeaux mixture 1% (if weather is humid/rainy)
- **45 DAS:** Panchagavya 3% + micronutrients
- **55 DAS onward:** Repeat based on weather and disease incidence (every 10-15 days)

### Vector Control (for Viral Diseases)

- **Neem oil spray:** 0.5% every 7 days
- **Yellow sticky traps:** 8-10 traps/acre
- **Reflective mulch:** Silver/white plastic mulch to repel insects
- **Border crops:** Plant tall crops like maize, sorghum as barrier
- **Rogue infected plants:** Remove immediately to prevent spread
