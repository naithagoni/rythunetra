## LAND TYPES IN TELANGANA

### Soil Classification Verified by Government Sources

#### 1. **Red Sandy Loams (Chalaka)**

- **Telugu Name:** ఎర్ర ఇసుక బంకమట్టి (Erra Isuka Banka Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia, Telangana Geography)
- **pH Range:** 5.5-7.0
- **Organic Matter:** Low to Medium (0.3-0.7%)
- **Water Retention:** Low to Moderate
- **Districts:** Adilabad, Nirmal, Mancherial, Karimnagar, Warangal, Khammam
- **Major Crops:** Cotton, Maize, Red Gram, Groundnut, Sunflower
- **Characteristics:** Well-drained, light texture, drought-prone

#### 2. **Red Loamy Sands (Dubba)**

- **Telugu Name:** ఎర్ర ఇసుక మట్టి (Erra Isuka Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia, Telangana Geography)
- **pH Range:** 5.0-6.5
- **Organic Matter:** Very Low (<0.3%)
- **Water Retention:** Very Low
- **Districts:** Adilabad, Mancherial, Khammam, Nalgonda (upland areas)
- **Major Crops:** Millets (Jowar, Bajra), Groundnut, Castor
- **Characteristics:** Sandy, poor fertility, requires irrigation

#### 3. **Black Cotton Soils (Very Deep)**

- **Telugu Name:** నల్ల పత్తి మట్టి (Nalla Patti Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia, Official Agricultural Reports)
- **pH Range:** 7.5-8.5
- **Organic Matter:** Medium to High (0.5-1.0%)
- **Water Retention:** Very High
- **Districts:** Nizamabad, Kamareddy, Medak, Siddipet, Sangareddy, Nagarkurnool, Wanaparthy
- **Major Crops:** Cotton, Soybean, Bengal Gram, Jowar, Sunflower
- **Characteristics:** High clay content, swells when wet, cracks when dry, fertile

#### 4. **Shallow to Medium Black Soils**

- **Telugu Name:** లోతు లేని నల్ల మట్టి (Lothu Leni Nalla Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia, Soil Survey)
- **pH Range:** 7.0-8.0
- **Organic Matter:** Medium (0.4-0.7%)
- **Water Retention:** Moderate to High
- **Districts:** Medak, Kamareddy, Mahabubnagar, Vikarabad
- **Major Crops:** Cotton, Red Gram, Green Gram, Safflower
- **Characteristics:** Less deep than very deep black soils, moderate fertility

#### 5. **Lateritic Soils**

- **Telugu Name:** ల్యాటరైట్ మట్టి (Laterite Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia Geography)
- **pH Range:** 4.5-6.0
- **Organic Matter:** Low (0.2-0.5%)
- **Water Retention:** Poor
- **Districts:** Adilabad (forest areas), Komaram Bheem Asifabad, Mancherial
- **Major Crops:** Cashew, Tamarind, Millets (Ragi, Bajra)
- **Characteristics:** Acidic, iron-rich, poor fertility, found in plateau regions

#### 6. **Alluvial Soils (Deltaic & Riverine)**

- **Telugu Name:** వరద మట్టి (Varada Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia, Agricultural Database)
- **pH Range:** 6.5-7.5
- **Organic Matter:** Medium to High (0.5-1.2%)
- **Water Retention:** High
- **Districts:** Khammam, Bhadradri Kothagudem, Mahabubabad, Jayashankar Bhupalpally (along Godavari basin)
- **Major Crops:** Paddy (Rice), Sugarcane, Turmeric, Vegetables
- **Characteristics:** Fertile, high nutrients, suitable for intensive cropping

#### 7. **Salt-Affected Soils (Saline & Alkaline)**

- **Telugu Name:** ఉప్పు మట్టి (Uppu Matti)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia Telangana Geography)
- **pH Range:** 8.5-10.0+
- **Organic Matter:** Very Low (<0.2%)
- **Water Retention:** Variable
- **Districts:** Nalgonda, Suryapet, Yadadri Bhuvanagiri (localized patches)
- **Major Crops:** Limited - Tolerant varieties of Paddy, Barley (experimental)
- **Characteristics:** High salinity/alkalinity, poor productivity, requires soil amendment
- **Remediation:** Gypsum application, organic matter addition, leaching with good drainage

## DISEASES IN TELANGANA

### Classification: VERIFIED vs NON-VERIFIED

**VERIFIED:** Diseases officially documented by PJTSAU, Agricultural Universities, ICAR institutes, disease diagnostic labs, Extension bulletins
**NON-VERIFIED:** Diseases reported by farmers, field observations, extension officers but not formally documented in research papers

### PADDY (RICE) DISEASES

#### VERIFIED DISEASES - Paddy

##### 1. **Rice Blast (Leaf, Neck, Nodal, Collar)**

- **Telugu Name:** వరి బ్లాస్ట్ వ్యాధి (Vari Blast Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Magnaporthe oryzae_ (formerly _Pyricularia grisea_)
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia - List of Rice Diseases)
- **Symptoms:**
    - White to gray-green lesions with darker borders on leaves
    - Elliptical/spindle-shaped older lesions
    - Neck rot (panicle neck breaking)
    - Nodal infection causing stem breakage
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (>90%), 25-28°C, long leaf wetness periods, excessive nitrogen
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Very common in Warangal, Karimnagar, Khammam, Nalgonda (rainy season)
- **Land Types Affected:** All (more severe in nitrogen-rich soils)
- **Severity:** HIGH - Can cause 50-100% yield loss
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma viride, Pseudomonas fluorescens, Neem oil, Panchagavya

##### 2. **Bacterial Blight**

- **Telugu Name:** బ్యాక్టీరియా ఆకుముడత వ్యాధి (Bacteria Aku Mudatha Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Xanthomonas oryzae_ pv. _oryzae_
- **Type:** Bacterial
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Water-soaked lesions on leaf edges
    - Yellowing and eventual drying (kresek stage in seedlings)
    - Milky bacterial ooze from cut stems
- **Favorable Conditions:** High temperature (25-34°C), high humidity, wounds, heavy rains
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in all paddy-growing areas, Kharif season
- **Land Types Affected:** All (severe in flood-prone alluvial soils)
- **Severity:** MEDIUM to HIGH
- **Organic Remedies:** Pseudomonas fluorescens seed treatment, Copper oxychloride (organic), Bordeaux mixture

##### 3. **Brown Spot**

- **Telugu Name:** గోధుమ రంగు మచ్చలు (Godhuma Rangu Machhalu)
- **Pathogen:** _Cochliobolus miyabeanus_ (_Bipolaris oryzae_ - anamorph)
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Circular to oval brown spots with gray/white centers
    - Affects leaves, leaf sheaths, panicles, grains (pecky rice)
    - Can cause seedling blight
- **Favorable Conditions:** Nutrient deficiency (especially K), drought stress, high humidity
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Very common - deficiency-prone soils, upland red soils
- **Land Types Affected:** Red sandy loams, nutrient-poor soils
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma viride, balanced nutrition (potash enhancement), Jeevamrutham

##### 4. **Sheath Blight**

- **Telugu Name:** ఆవరణ ముడత వ్యాధి (Aavarana Mudatha Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Rhizoctonia solani_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Oblong or ellipsoid greenish-gray lesions on leaf sheaths near water line
    - Lesions enlarge and coalesce
    - Can affect panicles in severe cases
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, dense planting, excessive nitrogen, temperatures 28-32°C
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in irrigated lowland areas - Warangal, Khammam, Nalgonda
- **Land Types Affected:** Irrigated alluvial and black soils
- **Severity:** MEDIUM to HIGH
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma harzianum, Pseudomonas fluorescens, plant spacing management

##### 5. **False Smut**

- **Telugu Name:** తప్పుడు పుట్ట వ్యాధి (Tappudu Putta Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Ustilaginoidea virens_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Individual grains transformed into velvety green/greenish-black balls
    - Spore balls larger than normal grains
    - Affects few grains per panicle
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity at flowering stage, temperatures 25-28°C, excessive nitrogen
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Increasing incidence in high-input irrigated areas
- **Land Types Affected:** Irrigated areas - alluvial and black soils
- **Severity:** LOW to MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Pseudomonas fluorescens, copper-based fungicides, balanced fertilization

##### 6. **Bacterial Leaf Streak**

- **Telugu Name:** బ్యాక్టీరియా ఆకు చారలు (Bacteria Aku Charalu)
- **Pathogen:** _Xanthomonas oryzae_ pv. _oryzicola_
- **Type:** Bacterial
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Narrow, dark green to brown interveinal streaks
    - Streaks merge to form necrotic areas
    - Different from bacterial blight (starts interveinal, not from edges)
- **Favorable Conditions:** High temperature, high humidity, windy conditions
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Sporadic occurrence, mainly in Kharif season
- **Land Types Affected:** All irrigated areas
- **Severity:** LOW to MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Copper spray, Pseudomonas fluorescens, resistant varieties

##### 7. **Sheath Rot**

- **Telugu Name:** ఆవరణ కుళ్ళు (Aavarana Kullu)
- **Pathogen:** _Sarocladium oryzae_ (_Acrocylindrium oryzae_)
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Wikipedia)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Oblong or irregular grayish-brown lesions on leaf sheaths
    - Boots (young panicles) may rot within sheath
    - Panicles may emerge partially or not at all
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, heavy nitrogen, dense planting
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in high-input areas
- **Land Types Affected:** Irrigated alluvial and black soils
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma viride, Pseudomonas, avoid excessive nitrogen

#### NON-VERIFIED DISEASES - Paddy (Farmer/Field Reports)

##### 8. **Root Rot (Seedling Stage)**

- **Telugu Name:** వేరు కుళ్ళు (Veeru Kullu)

- **Status:** NON-VERIFIED (Field observations, no formal diagnostic data)

- **Land Types Affected:** Poor-drainage areas

##### 9. **Grain Discoloration (Dirty Panicle)**

- **Telugu Name:** గింజల రంగు మార్పు (Ginjala Rangu Marpu)

- **Type:** Fungal/Bacterial complex
- **Status:** NON-VERIFIED (Field problem, not well-documented pathogen-wise)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Dark brown to blackish discoloration of grains

- **Land Types Affected:** All types

### COTTON DISEASES

#### VERIFIED DISEASES - Cotton

##### 10. **Cotton Wilt (Fusarium Wilt)**

- **Telugu Name:** పత్తి వాడిపోవు వ్యాధి (Patti Vaadipovu Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Fusarium oxysporum_ f.sp. _vasinfectum_
- **Type:** Fungal (soil-borne)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Major disease in India, ICAR documented)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Sudden wilting of plants at any growth stage
    - Yellowing of leaves (one side first)
    - Vascular browning (brown streaks in stem when cut)
    - Plant death
- **Favorable Conditions:** 25-30°C, acidic soils, nematode injury
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Very common - all cotton-growing districts (Adilabad, Warangal, Karimnagar, Nalgonda)
- **Land Types Affected:** Red sandy loams, black soils
- **Severity:** HIGH - Can cause 10-50% crop loss
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma viride seed/soil treatment, Pseudomonas fluorescens, crop rotation

##### 11. **Bacterial Blight of Cotton**

- **Telugu Name:** పత్తి బ్యాక్టీరియా వ్యాధి (Patti Bacteria Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Xanthomonas citri_ pv. _malvacearum_
- **Type:** Bacterial
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Common worldwide cotton disease)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Angular water-soaked lesions on leaves (vein-limited)
    - Black arm (stem cankers)
    - Boll rot
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, rainfall, hail/wind damage
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in Kharif season, rainy periods
- **Land Types Affected:** All cotton-growing soils
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Copper-based sprays, Pseudomonas fluorescens, resistant varieties

##### 12. **Grey Mildew (Ashy Mildew)**

- **Telugu Name:** బూడిద ఆకు తెగులు (Boodida Aku Tegulu)
- **Pathogen:** _Ramularia areola_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Common in Telangana cotton)
- **Symptoms:**
    - White powdery growth on lower leaf surface
    - Yellow patches on upper surface
    - Premature defoliation
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, moderate temperature (20-28°C), cloudy weather
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in September-October (post-monsoon)
- **Land Types Affected:** All types
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Organic Remedies:** Neem oil + Panchagavya spray, sulfur dust

##### 13. **Alternaria Leaf Spot**

- **Telugu Name:** ఆల్టర్నేరియా ఆకు మచ్చ (Alternaria Aku Machha)
- **Pathogen:** _Alternaria macrospora_, _A. alternata_
- **Type:** Fungal

- **Symptoms:**
    - Circular to irregular brown spots with concentric rings
    - Premature leaf drop
    - Boll infection in severe cases
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, warm temperature
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in Karimnagar, Warangal, Adilabad
- **Land Types Affected:** All types

- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma spray, Neem oil, Panchagavya

### CHILLI DISEASES

#### VERIFIED DISEASES - Chilli

##### 15. **Chilli Leaf Curl Virus**

- **Telugu Name:** మిరపకాయ ఆకు ముడత వైరస్ (Mirapakaya Aku Mudatha Virus)
- **Pathogen:** Begomovirus (transmitted by whitefly _Bemisia tabaci_)
- **Type:** Viral
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Major disease in India)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Severe upward curling of leaves
    - Yellowing
    - Stunted plant growth
    - Reduced fruiting
- **Favorable Conditions:** High whitefly population, dry weather
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Very common - Warangal, Khammam, Mahabubnagar
- **Land Types Affected:** All types
- **Severity:** VERY HIGH - Can cause total crop failure
- **Organic Remedies:** Neem oil for vector control, removal of infected plants, border crops (marigold), reflective mulch

##### 16. **Anthracnose (Fruit Rot)**

- **Telugu Name:** కాయ కుళ్ళు వ్యాధి (Kaaya Kullu Vyadhi)
- **Pathogen:** _Colletotrichum capsici_, _C. gloeosporioides_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Common worldwide)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Circular sunken lesions on fruits
    - Dark brown to black spots
    - Pink/orange spore masses in humid conditions
    - Pre and post-harvest fruit rot
- **Favorable Conditions:** High humidity, rainfall, temperatures 25-30°C
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Very common in rainy season
- **Land Types Affected:** All types
- **Severity:** HIGH (Post-harvest losses)
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma viride spray, Garlic bulb extract, proper drying

##### 17. **Damping Off (Seedling Disease)**

- **Telugu Name:** మొలక కుళ్ళు (Molaka Kullu)
- **Pathogen:** _Pythium_ spp., _Rhizoctonia solani_, _Fusarium_ spp.
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Nursery disease)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Post-emergence: Water-soaked stems at soil line, toppling of seedlings
    - Pre-emergence: Seeds/young seedlings rot before emergence
- **Favorable Conditions:** Excess moisture, high seed density, poor drainage
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in nurseries across all districts
- **Land Types Affected:** Nursery beds (all soil types)
- **Severity:** MEDIUM to HIGH (Nursery stage)
- **Organic Remedies:** Trichoderma seed treatment, proper drainage, soil solarization

##### 18. **Powdery Mildew**

- **Telugu Name:** తెల్లపొడి తెగులు (Tellapodi Tegulu)
- **Pathogen:** _Leveillula taurica_
- **Type:** Fungal
- **Status:** VERIFIED
- **Symptoms:**
    - White powdery growth on leaves (mostly lower surface)
    - Yellowing and distortion
    - Premature defoliation
- **Favorable Conditions:** Moderate temperature (20-30°C), high humidity (but dry leaf surface)
- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common in October-November (post-monsoon)

### VEGETABLES DISEASE DOCUMENTATION PATTERN

**CRITICAL PATTERN OBSERVED:** ALL 16 vegetables researched (100%) systematically lack comprehensive Wikipedia disease lists ("List of [Vegetable] Diseases" pages) despite:

**Production Volumes:** Cabbage 74M tonnes, Watermelon 105M, Cucumber 91M, Onion 106M, Tomato 186M tonnes globally

**Wikipedia Quality:** Cabbage is Featured Article with comprehensive production/nutrition/cultivation coverage

**Nutritional Importance:** Bell pepper richest vitamin C vegetable (158% DV), Carrot richest vitamin A (334% DV), Pumpkin 558% DV beta-carotene

**Historical Significance:** Pumpkin oldest domesticated Americas (7,000 BC), Bottle gourd 10,000+ years (Africa to Asia 8000 BCE)

**Safety Concerns:** Bottle gourd LETHAL cucurbitacin poisoning (deaths documented), Bitter melon pregnancy contraindication (abortifacient)

**Agricultural Importance:** Yellow vein mosaic devastating okra, Bacterial wilt major tomato/brinjal constraint

**REASON FOR PATTERN:** Wikipedia disease list coverage reflects **historic phytopathological research investment priorities** - major field crops (cereals, pulses, commercial crops like cotton/tobacco/sugarcane), major oilseeds, and certain fruits received comprehensive disease cataloging over decades of agricultural research. Vegetables, despite current economic/nutritional/production importance, were systematically excluded from comprehensive disease list documentation on Wikipedia.

**HOWEVER:** Vegetable main articles DO contain varying amounts of disease/pest information (Cabbage 10 diseases/13 pests, Cauliflower 5/5, Okra 4, Watermelon 3, many others 0).

### POTATO DISEASES

#### VERIFIED DISEASES - Potato

##### 136. **Late Blight**

- **Telugu Name:** ఆలస్య తెగులు (Alasya Tegulu)

- **Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)
- **Status:** VERIFIED (Major disease worldwide)

- **Status:** VERIFIED (Common disease)
- **Symptoms:**
    - Circular brown spots with concentric rings (target board pattern) on older leaves
    - Yellowing of leaves around spots, premature leaf drop

- **Occurrence in Telangana:** Common throughout growing season
- **Land Types Affected:** All soil types
- **Severity:** MEDIUM to HIGH
- **Organic Remedies:** Neem Oil 0.5-1% spray (5-10ml/L) every 7-10 days, Panchagavya 3% foliar spray (30ml/L) for immunity, Copper Oxychloride 0.3% (3g/L) as contact fungicide

- **Status:** VERIFIED (Major bacterial disease)
- **Symptoms:**

- **Severity:** VERY HIGH - No cure once infected
